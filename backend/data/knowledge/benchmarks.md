# Contact Center Benchmark Reference (Retail Banking)

## Inbound Call Handling
- Median AHT (Average Handle Time) for retail banking voice: 280-340 seconds. Above 380s indicates poor knowledge tooling or excessive transfers.
- IVR containment/deflection best-in-class: 40-55%. Below 25% means the IVR is menu-only with no conversational self-service.
- Call transfer rate best-in-class: under 8%. High transfers correlate with poor skills-based routing.

## First Contact Resolution (FCR)
- Industry median FCR: 70-75%. Top quartile: 80%+.
- Each 1% FCR improvement reduces repeat-call volume ~1%, saving roughly cost_per_call x monthly_calls x 0.01 annually.
- Root causes of low FCR: no 360 customer view at desktop, no screen-pop from CRM, agents lack real-time guidance.

## Automation & AI
- Conversational IVR (NLU-based) lifts deflection 15-25 points over DTMF menus.
- Real-time agent assist (e.g. Cresta, Cogito, Balto) reduces AHT 8-15% and improves QA consistency.
- Automated post-call summarization saves 30-60 seconds of after-call work per interaction.

## Quality Assurance
- Manual QA typically samples 1-3% of calls. AI-driven QA (Observe.AI, Tethr) scores 100% of interactions.
- Moving from 2% manual sampling to 100% automated QA surfaces compliance risk and coaching opportunities at scale.

## Agent Experience & Attrition
- Contact center attrition median: 30-45%. Each replaced agent costs $10k-$20k in hiring + ramp.
- Knowledge tooling and real-time assist are the top two levers for reducing agent effort and attrition.

## CSAT
- Voice CSAT median: 75-80%. Below 75% signals routing, wait-time, or resolution problems.
